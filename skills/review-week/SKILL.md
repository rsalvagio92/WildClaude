# Weekly Review

Sunday ritual. 15-20 minutes. Honest accounting of the week, clear intentions for next.

## Step 1: Read the week's context

Before asking anything, pull:
- `life/goals/_kernel/key.md` — active goals and milestones
- Last 7 entries in `life/me/_kernel/log.md` — evening logs, focus sessions, journal entries

Summarize what you can see: what was logged, what got done, energy trends.

## Step 2: Run the scorecard

Present a structured review. Ask each section, one at a time (or show all and ask for input):

### Goals progress
For each active goal in `key.md`:
- What moved forward this week?
- What's the current milestone status?
- Any blockers?

### Habits and energy
- Average energy this week (pull from evening log entries if available)
- Any habits that were consistent or broke down?

### Wins
"What are 3 things from this week you can actually feel good about?"
(Push for 3 even if the week felt bad. There's always something.)

### Lessons
"What's one thing you'd do differently? One thing you learned?"

## Step 3: Plan next week's top 3

Based on goals, open items, and what came up in the review:

Ask: "What are the 3 most important things for next week?"

Don't just carry over this week's list — sanity-check against goal priorities.

## Step 4: Log the review

Prepend to `life/me/_kernel/log.md`:

```
## [YYYY-MM-DD] Weekly Review

**Goals pulse:**
- [goal]: [status note]

**Wins:**
1. [win]
2. [win]
3. [win]

**Lesson:** [one lesson]
**Energy avg:** [N/10 or "mixed"]

**Next week's top 3:**
1. [priority]
2. [priority]
3. [priority]
```

## Rules

- Never skip the wins section — force at least one
- Don't let "nothing happened" close the review without asking twice
- The top 3 for next week should connect back to active goals, not just reactive tasks
- Keep the log entry concise — it's a record, not an essay
